About Varanasi Ropeway Project

The Varanasi Ropeway Project is India's first urban public ropeway system. It connects Cantt Railway Station to Godowlia to reduce traffic congestion and provide faster, eco-friendly transportation for residents and tourists.

Project Timeline

  • Project approved by the Government of India and Uttar Pradesh.
  • Construction began with station and pillar development.
  • Testing and final work are expected before public opening.

Architecture & Design

  • Modern monocable detachable gondola system.
  • Five passenger stations.
  • Automatic gondola cabins with safety systems.
  • Designed for quick urban transport.

Project Cost

The total estimated project cost is around ₹645 crore, including stations, ropeway system, civil works and supporting infrastructure.

Facilities

The ropeway will offer automatic cabins, CCTV surveillance, disabled-friendly access, digital ticketing, modern stations and comfortable city travel.

Expected Completion

  • Status: Final construction and testing stage
  • Expected Opening: After safety approvals
  • Route Length: About 3.75 km
  • Stations: 5

How to Reach

  • Start: Cantt Railway Station
  • End: Godowlia
  • Nearest Airport: Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport
  • Access: Easily reachable by road and rail.

Nearby Attractions

  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple
  • Godowlia
  • Dashashwamedh Ghat
  • Assi Ghat
  • Sarnath

Travel Tips & Fun Facts

  • Use the ropeway to avoid traffic.
  • Visit early during festivals.
  • Check operating hours before travel.
